# New York State Court and Legal AI Materials

Research date: July 19, 2026

Scope: New York State Unified Court System policy materials, rulemaking, advisory materials, and New York state court decisions involving artificial intelligence, generative AI, hallucinated authorities, AI-assisted filings, discovery into AI use, AI-generated/suspected evidence, and professional responsibility.

Source preference: official New York State Unified Court System and New York State Law Reporting Bureau sources. Secondary sources were used only as search leads and are not relied on as authority unless expressly noted.

## Executive Signals

- New York now has a statewide court rule: 22 NYCRR Part 161, effective June 1, 2026, permits AI use for court papers but keeps responsibility on the filer and discourages routine disclosure requirements.
- The statewide model rule focuses on human verification rather than blanket bans: users must understand AI limitations and independently ensure no fabricated or fictitious cases, statutes, or other material are submitted.
- UCS has a separate internal AI policy for judges and court personnel, effective October 2025, focused on responsible use, fairness, accountability, security, and confidentiality.
- New York appellate courts are actively sanctioning AI-related hallucinated authority. The strongest appellate examples are Deutsche Bank Natl. Trust Co. v LeTennier, Matter of Julien v Arthur, and Landberg v City of New York.
- Trial courts are also developing practical controls: certification requirements, striking papers, sanctions hearings, referrals, warnings, and refusal to let parties "redo" filings after hallucinated authorities are exposed.
- AI is not only a legal-writing issue. Assini addresses discovery into OpenAI/ChatGPT account materials and work-product/privacy concerns; Sanchez flags possibly AI-generated or fabricated deposition testimony; Matter of Weber addresses AI-generated evidence.

### 22 NYCRR Part 161 / AO 75/26

- Title: Part 161. Use of Artificial Intelligence Technology; AO/75/26
- Authority: Chief Administrative Judge of the Courts / Rules of the Chief Administrator
- Date: Adopted March 25, 2026; effective June 1, 2026
- Source URLs: https://www.nycourts.gov/rules/part-161-use-artificial-intelligence-technology and https://www.nycourts.gov/LegacyPDFS/rules/comments/pdf/AdministrativeOrder-CAJ-75-2026-ArttificialIntelligence-032526r.pdf
- Official primary source: Yes
- Legal issue: Statewide policy for AI use by attorneys and parties in papers submitted in civil and criminal courts.
- Practical signal: AI use in preparing court papers is not prohibited. Routine disclosure of AI use is discouraged because ordinary duties already apply to all submissions. Courts may adopt a local part rule, but are encouraged to use the model rule. The model rule requires careful review and independent verification that filings contain no fabricated or fictitious cases, statutes, or other material.
- Suggested tags/legal areas: court rules; civil procedure; criminal procedure; legal ethics; sanctions; generative AI; legal writing.

## New York State Decisions

### Matter of Samuel

- Citation/title: Matter of Samuel, 2024 NY Slip Op 24014 [82 Misc 3d 616]
- Court/authority: Surrogate's Court, Kings County; Graham, S.
- Date: January 11, 2024
- Source URL: https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/3dseries/2024/2024_24014.htm
- Official primary source: Yes - New York State Law Reporting Bureau
- Legal issue: Attorney reply papers allegedly contained fictional or erroneous citations from a website containing generative-AI-created information.
- Practical signal: Early New York state AI hallucination decision; court treated fake citations as a sanctions/professional-responsibility issue and struck/scrutinized offending papers.
- Suggested tags/legal areas: trusts and estates; Surrogate's Court; sanctions; hallucinated authority; professional responsibility; generative AI.

### Matter of Weber

- Citation/title: Matter of Weber, 2024 NY Slip Op 24258 [85 Misc 3d 727]
- Court/authority: Surrogate's Court, Erie County
- Date: October 2024
- Source URL: https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/3dseries/2024/2024_24258.htm
- Official primary source: Yes - New York State Law Reporting Bureau

### Ader v Ader

- Citation/title: Ader v Ader, 2025 NY Slip Op 51563(U) [87 Misc 3d 1213(A)] and related 2025 NY Slip Op 33045(U)
- Court/authority: Supreme Court, New York County; Cohen, J.
- Date: October 1, 2025 for reported sanctions decision; July 29, 2025 related decision
- Source URLs: https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/3dseries/2025/2025_51563.htm and https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/pdfs/2025/2025_33045.pdf
- Official primary source: Yes - New York State Law Reporting Bureau / UCS reposted NYSCEF decision
- Legal issue: Unvetted AI use in motion practice; hallucinated citations and quotations in summary judgment and sanctions briefing.
- Practical signal: Important trial-court sanctions precedent. The court rejected the idea that fake authorities can be excused because real authority might support the same broad point; continued hallucinations in sanctions opposition aggravate the conduct.
- Suggested tags/legal areas: commercial litigation; sanctions; attorney fees; hallucinated authority; legal ethics; supervision.

### Grymes Dev. Co. v Fodera

### Deutsche Bank Natl. Trust Co. v LeTennier

- Citation/title: Deutsche Bank Natl. Trust Co. v LeTennier, 2026 NY Slip Op 00040
- Court/authority: Appellate Division, Third Department; Fisher, J.
- Date: January 8, 2026
- Source URL: https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/3dseries/2026/2026_00040.htm
- Official primary source: Yes - New York State Law Reporting Bureau
- Legal issue: Appellate brief and later filings contained fabricated cases and false legal propositions believed to be AI-generated.
- Practical signal: Leading NY appellate sanctions framework. The Third Department imposed monetary sanctions, treated fabricated authorities as frivolous conduct under 22 NYCRR 130-1.1, and identified factors including number of fake authorities, fake quotes, continuation after notice, admission/remorse, and impact on proceedings.
- Suggested tags/legal areas: appellate practice; foreclosure; sanctions; 22 NYCRR 130-1.1; GenAI; hallucinated authority; pro se/attorney conduct.

### Cassata v Michael Macrina Architect, P.C.

- Citation/title: Cassata v Michael Macrina Architect, P.C., 2026 NY Slip Op 26014

- Date: February 2026
- Source URL: https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/3dseries/2026/2026_26014.htm

### Landberg v City of New York

- Citation/title: Landberg v City of New York, 2026 NY Slip Op 03935
- Court/authority: Appellate Division, Second Department; LaSalle, P.J.
- Date: June 23, 2026
- Source URL: https://www.nycourts.gov/reporter/current/3dseries/2026/2026_03935.shtml
