# NYC / New York AI Regulation Research Report - Legislation and Agencies

Agent: NY-AEDT-AGENCIES
Research date: 2026-07-19
Scope note: Non-case-law New York City and New York State AI regulation, legislation, agency guidance, official reports, and monitoring sources. Case law is intentionally excluded except where court administration/agency policy is the source.

## Executive Signals

- NYC Local Law 144 is in force and DCWP states enforcement began July 5, 2023. The operative compliance surface remains bias audit, public audit-summary posting, and candidate/employee notices before AEDT use.
- NYC AEDT rulemaking is mature, but monitoring should continue for DCWP FAQ/slides updates, NYC Rules penalty schedule changes, and Council amendments such as Int. 1066-2024 on AI impacts in civil service.
- NYC public-sector AI governance is now broader than LL144: OTI runs Local Law 35 algorithmic-tool reporting, AI Action Plan progress reporting, GenAI use guidance, and city AI principles/definitions.
- New York State moved beyond proposals with the RAISE Act signature announcement in December 2025; chapter-amendment/implementation monitoring now matters, especially the DFS-based oversight office for frontier-model transparency.
- The State legislative pipeline is active across public-sector automated decision-making, algorithmic discrimination, employment, banking/lending, surveillance pricing, health utilization review, therapy/psychotherapy AI, professional-impersonation chatbots, and AI-generated news/content disclosures.
- DFS is the strongest live sector regulator: final insurance AI/ECDIS guidance and AI-cybersecurity guidance impose practical governance expectations through existing insurance and cybersecurity authority.
- NYSED, DOH, and NY Courts have legally significant guidance/policy activity, even where not general AI statutes.

## Core NYC AEDT Sources

### 1. Local Law 144 of 2021 - Automated Employment Decision Tools

- Title: Local Law 144 / Automated Employment Decision Tools.
- Authority: NYC Council; codified in NYC Administrative Code sections 20-870 et seq.; enforced by NYC Department of Consumer and Worker Protection (DCWP).
- Date/status: Enacted 2021; DCWP states enforcement of the law and rule began July 5, 2023.
- Source URL: https://www.nyc.gov/site/dca/about/automated-employment-decision-tools.page
- Official primary source?: Yes.
- Legal issue: Employers and employment agencies may not use covered AEDTs to screen NYC candidates/employees for hiring or promotion unless a qualifying bias audit occurred within one year, audit-summary information is public, and required notices are given.
- Practical signal: Any employer, staffing firm, vendor, or counsel dealing with NYC hiring tools should treat AEDT compliance as a live pre-use checklist, not a post-hoc defense.
- Suggested tags/legal areas: `employment`, `algorithmic-bias`, `audit`, `notice`, `nyc-dcwp`, `local-law-144`, `hr-tech`.
- Monitoring cadence/keywords: Monthly; keywords `Automated Employment Decision Tools`, `AEDT`, `bias audit`, `Local Law 144`, `DCWP FAQ`, `employment agency complaint`.
