# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary: legal professionals, regulators, and sophisticated clients who need trustworthy, current AI-law intelligence — they arrive to *find* something (a development, a jurisdiction's status, a verified source, a note) and judge credibility fast. Secondary: the attorney/editor operating the admin review workflow (monitoring, verifying, and publishing items). Context of use: focused, often skeptical reading on desktop and mobile; users weigh whether a claim is binding law vs. governance posture before acting.

## Product Purpose

An attorney-led AI Law & Legal Intelligence platform for **C. Saint-Girons, Esq**. It combines a professional identity, an editorial notes/commentary layer, and an AI Regulation Monitor (multi-country legal/news ingestion with mandatory human review before publication). Success = a visitor quickly understands what changed, where, and how reliable it is, and trusts the source enough to rely on it. The monitor is one feature among several; the site must not be reduced to it.

## Brand Personality

Futuristic and innovative legal-tech, carried with the restraint of a serious attorney. Voice: precise, sourced, never hyperbolic. It should feel modern and forward-looking (AI-native) while signaling authority and trust. Three words: **forward, precise, credible.**

## Anti-references

- **Flashy SaaS startup** (the explicit anti-reference): no purple gradients, emoji, "Get started free" energy, inflated marketing claims, or growth-hack UI.
- Generic navy-and-serif law-firm template.
- Neon/crypto "hacker" tech aesthetic and decorative glow.
- Cognitive overload: walls of text, identical card grids, everything at one visual level.

## Design Principles

1. **Credibility over spectacle** — every visual choice must read as trustworthy to a skeptical legal reader; effects support professionalism, never distract from it.
2. **Show provenance, not assertion** — surface source, date, and verification status; distinguish binding law from guidance/soft law/standards by design.
3. **Intuitive first** — the user's job (find and judge a development) is one or two clear moves away; structure and hierarchy do the work before copy does.
4. **Restrained motion** — purposeful, fast, eased; honors reduced-motion. Motion signals quality, never demands attention.
5. **Futuristic but quiet** — AI-native modernity expressed through typography, light/glass surfaces, and precise spacing, not loud color or gimmicks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body contrast ≥4.5:1 (large text ≥3:1), visible keyboard focus (on-brand focus ring), full keyboard navigation, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Avoid conveying status (verified / binding / live) by color alone — pair with label or icon.
